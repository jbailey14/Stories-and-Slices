1/30/2017 - Maids of Misfortune: A Victorian San Francisco Mystery

Another book review comin' at cha:

I read Maids of Misfortune which is a mystery/suspense novel set in the late 1800's where a woman who is a financial consultant has a client who "commits suicide." She knows him well enough to believe it was murder and even goes so far as to pretend to be a housemaid in the deceased's home to figure out who did it.

This is what I would call a cozy mystery. The book is a page-turner, but it isn't much in the thriller category. I quite enjoyed the story line and the author's writing style. I found myself flying through it and to be almost finished before I even knew it. I will be picking up the other Victorian mysteries by the same author.

I highly recommend this book for those who love the game Clue or the TV show Murder She Wrote. This will be right up your alley.
